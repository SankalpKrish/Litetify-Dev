# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Spotify Premium users who want Spicetify-level customizability without Electron bloat. They are technically inclined, value performance and personalization, and choose tools that feel purpose-built rather than generic. Primary context: sitting at a desktop, listening to music, managing their library and playlists, with the app in the background or as the active window. Secondary context: browsing and installing mods/themes to shape the experience to their taste.

## Product Purpose

A lightweight, performant, moddable Spotify Premium desktop client. Smaller and faster than the official Electron app, with a Spicetify-style mod system for themes, extensions, and custom apps. Success looks like users choosing Litetify not just because it's lighter, but because it feels better — more responsive, more personal, more intentional.

## Brand Personality

Bold, creative, energetic, authentic — mirroring the Spotify brand personality without imitating the official client. The interface should feel like it belongs in the Spotify ecosystem: musically charged, confident, human. Not corporate, not gaudy, not derivative.

## Anti-references

- The official Spotify desktop app: bloated, slow, heavy, cluttered. Litetify is the athletic alternative.
- Generic Electron apps / SaaS-cream aesthetics: bland, corporate, indistinguishable. Litetify has character.
- Over-designed / gamer / RGB music players: no skins, no visual noise. Premium restraint.

## Design Principles

1. **Music first, chrome second.** Every UI element either serves the music or gets out of its way. Album art, track info, and playback controls take priority. Navigation, settings, and chrome recede.
2. **Belong without imitating.** The interface feels native to the Spotify ecosystem — same energy, same creative confidence — without copying the official client's layout, patterns, or visual weight.
3. **Performance is felt, not measured.** Transitions are instant, scrolling is buttery, the UI never lags behind the music. The speed of the tool is part of the experience, visible in every interaction.
4. **Moddable by default.** The base UI is clean, opinionated, and complete. But every surface is designed to welcome personalization — themes can reach in, swap tokens, replace components, without fighting the defaults.
5. **Lean without being spartan.** Minimalism serves clarity, not emptiness. Whitespace has purpose. Dense screens earn their density. Nothing is removed for the sake of minimalism if it hurts usability.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard good practices: keyboard navigation, semantic markup, sufficient contrast, screen reader labels. No formal WCAG level target at this stage, but AA compliance is the aspiration.
